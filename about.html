About The Tank Guide & FishKeepingLifeCo

Our mission is simple: to make aquarium care easy, scientific, and accessible for every beginner.

The Tank Guide is an educational website created by FishKeepingLifeCo, a small independent studio that loves freshwater aquariums. We build tools, books, and guides that turn confusing fishkeeping advice into clear steps you can follow with confidence.

What is The Tank Guide?

The Tank Guide is a home for beginner-friendly aquarium help. Instead of guessing at water changes or stocking rules, you can use our tools and guides to plan a healthy tank from the start.

  • Stocking Advisor – helps you think through fish compatibility, bioload, and tank size instead of using “one inch per gallon”.
  • Cycling Coach – walks you through the nitrogen cycle so you don’t rush fish into an uncycled tank.
  • Gear Guide – explains filters, heaters, media, and other equipment in plain language.
  • Daily Tank Journal & Dashboard – encourages small, consistent habits by tracking water tests, maintenance, and dosing.
  • Feature Your Tank – lets you share your aquarium story so other beginners can learn from real-world setups.

All of these live under one roof so new fishkeepers don’t have to piece together random advice from a dozen different sites.

Who Runs The Tank Guide?

The Tank Guide is created and maintained by FishKeepingLifeCo (Founder), a one-person studio that lives and learns through real home aquariums.

Over the years, that has meant full-time work, parenting, late-night water changes, and slowly building better habits one tank at a time. Instead of pretending everything is perfect, The Tank Guide is honest about mistakes, do-overs, and what it really takes to keep fish healthy in everyday life.

On the site and in our books, you’ll see “we” used a lot. That “we” includes the founder, the tanks themselves, and the growing community of readers who share feedback, questions, and featured setups.

Books & Learning Tools

Alongside the website, FishKeepingLifeCo publishes aquarium books and journals that match the same teaching style you see here.

  • Life in Balance: The Hidden Magic of Aquariums – an illustrated storybook that explains how healthy tanks really work behind the glass.
  • Daily Tank Journals – guided logbooks that help you track water parameters, maintenance, and patterns over time.
  • Coloring Books & Activity Pages – creative ways for kids and families to learn aquarium science together.

The goal is always the same: take real aquarium science and present it in a way that feels clear, calm, and doable.

What We Believe

  • Fish welfare first. A “pretty tank” only matters if the animals inside it are calm, healthy, and respected.
  • No gatekeeping. Beginners deserve real explanations, not “because I said so” answers.
  • Science, but human-sized. We use real aquarium chemistry and biology, but always translate it into plain language and simple actions.
  • Learn out loud. Mistakes and do-overs are shared so others can avoid the same problems.
  • Small habits win. Consistent water changes, testing, and observation matter more than expensive gadgets.

Who The Tank Guide is For

This site is built for:

  • Beginners setting up their very first freshwater tank.
  • Parents helping kids care for a betta or community aquarium.
  • Hobbyists coming back to fishkeeping after a break.
  • Anyone who wants calm, step-by-step help instead of conflicting advice.

If you’ve ever felt overwhelmed by cycling, stocking charts, or water parameters, you’re in the right place.

Quick Answers

Is The Tank Guide free to use?
Yes. The main tools, guides, and educational pages are free to access. Some books and journals are available for purchase to support the project.
Do you earn money from links or recommendations?
Sometimes. When you see a product recommendation, we may use affiliate links. Those links never change our guidance on whether something is actually a good fit for your tank.
Can I ask questions or share my tank?
Yes. You can use the contact form to send feedback or questions, and the “Feature Your Tank” page to submit your aquarium for possible inclusion in future content.
