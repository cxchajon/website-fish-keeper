Betta Fish in a Community Tank: Peacekeeping, Tank Mates & Aquascaping

Building a thriving community around a betta is absolutely possible when you plan for territories, keep the flow gentle, and choose the right neighbors. This is the full playbook I use to keep my bettas calm, colorful, and stress-free in planted tanks.

Start with the right footprint

Ten-gallon kits get all the attention, but a community betta really settles in once you step up to 20 gallons or more. The extra horizontal space lets schooling fish pass through without spooking your betta, and it makes it easier to build multiple territories. Pick a long tank over a tall cube so everyone has room to dodge.

Layer the substrate in two zones: nutrient-rich planting soil in the back half for stems and swords, and a softer sand foreground for corydoras or loaches. Keep everyone fed without overdoing it

Bettas learn quickly that community tanks mean more food, so split rations into two or three micro-meals. Combine floating betta pellets with a sinking staple for the schoolers so everyone eats at their own level. Fast the entire tank one day per week and swap in frozen daphnia or baby brine shrimp for variety.
